WHAT IS STREET ART?

Street art is visual art created in public spaces. In India it goes far beyond decoration — it is protest, celebration, identity and storytelling painted directly onto the walls of our cities for everyone to see, for free, every single day.

WHY IT MATTERS

Indian street art gives voice to the voiceless. From political murals in Delhi to feminist art in Mumbai, these walls speak what society often cannot say out loud. Art on the street belongs to everyone — it cannot be bought or locked away.

INDIA'S STORY

India has one of the most vibrant and fast growing street art scenes in Asia. From Lodhi Colony in Delhi to the chawls of Dharavi in Mumbai, colour is slowly but powerfully reclaiming the urban landscape of this country.

FEATURED ARTISTS

Daku

Delhi

Typography · Concept Art

Delhi-based typographic artist working anonymously. Famous for using Devanagari script as visual art — treating Hindi words as both message and medium. His work is philosophical, raw, and deeply Indian.

YantrArt

Mumbai, Maharashtra

Graffiti · Lettering · Street Murals

YantrArt is a Mumbai-based graffiti artist and muralist, known for his stunning blend of geometric patterns, bold lettering, and urban storytelling. He has pioneered large-scale street murals across India, bringing a distinct Indian voice to the global graffiti scene.

Harshvardhan Kadam

Pune, Maharashtra

Street Art · Urban Murals · Illustration

Inkbrushnme is a Pune‑based artist known for expansive mythological murals, blending traditional Indian narratives with bold public art. He leads street art developments across cities and has created some of India’s largest murals in public spaces.
