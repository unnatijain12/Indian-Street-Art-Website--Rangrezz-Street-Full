THE LANGUAGE OF THE WALLS

Every mark on a wall tells you something about the artist who made it. Street art and graffiti have developed a rich and complex visual vocabulary over decades — a set of distinct styles, each with its own rules, history, techniques, and culture. Learning to read these styles is like learning a language. Once you know what you are looking at, every wall becomes a conversation.

TAGS

Beginner

A tag is the most basic and fundamental unit of graffiti — your name written in a quick stylised signature...

  • Single colour only
  • Done extremely quickly
  • Artist name or alias only
  • Most common worldwide

THROW UPS

Beginner — Intermediate

A throw up is the next step up from a tag — a quick two colour piece in bubble letters...

  • Two colours maximum
  • Bubble letter style
  • Larger than a tag
  • Prioritises speed

BLOCKBUSTERS

Intermediate

Massive simple block letters designed to cover entire walls...

  • Very large scale
  • Simple rectangular letters
  • Often collaborative
  • Legibility over complexity

WILDSTYLE

Advanced

The most complex and technically demanding lettering style...

  • Extremely complex letters
  • Arrows and spikes
  • Multiple colours
  • Highest status style

STENCIL ART

Beginner — Intermediate

Uses pre-cut templates to apply images quickly and precisely...

  • Repeatable images
  • Fast execution
  • Political association

MURALS

Intermediate — Advanced

Large scale, planned artworks painted on walls...

  • Entire building facades
  • Highly detailed
  • Usually legal commissions

CALLIGRAFFITI

Advanced

Fusion of calligraphy and graffiti — powerful in Indian context...

  • Flowing elegant forms
  • Uses non-Latin scripts
  • Deep cultural identity

WHEATPASTE

Beginner

Printed artwork pasted onto walls using flour paste...

  • Paper-based
  • Studio created
  • Ages naturally

"Style is everything in graffiti. Anybody can make a mark. Only an artist can make it mean something."

— Yantr, Delhi
