WHERE THE WALLS COME ALIVE

Every year across India, blank walls are transformed into masterpieces over the course of just a few days. Street art festivals bring together Indian and international artists, local communities, and curious visitors in a shared celebration of public art.

These are not gallery openings behind closed doors — they happen on the streets, in full public view, and anyone can watch an artist work.

Here is your guide to the most important street art festivals and events happening across India.

UPCOMING

St+art Delhi Festival 2024

15 November — 30 November 2024

Lodhi Colony, New Delhi

The annual St+art Delhi Festival returns to Lodhi Colony for its tenth edition. Over 20 Indian and international artists will paint new large scale murals across the colony over two weeks.

  • 20+ new murals painted live
  • Artist talks and walkthroughs
  • Photography walks
  • Free entry

Organiser: St+art India Foundation

UPCOMING

Sassoon Docks Art Project 2024

1 December — 15 December 2024

Sassoon Docks, Colaba, Mumbai

Mumbai's most unique art festival returns to the historic Sassoon Docks fish market, transforming a working dock into a world class outdoor gallery.

  • Murals on working boats
  • Live public sessions
  • Evening events
  • Food stalls

Organiser: St+art India Foundation & Mumbai Port Trust

UPCOMING

Bengaluru Street Art Festival

10 January — 25 January 2025

Indiranagar, Bengaluru

Bengaluru's annual street art festival exploring Urban Nature and the relationship between the city and the environment.

  • 15+ murals
  • Community mural
  • Panel discussions

Organiser: Rang De Bengaluru Collective

UPCOMING

Jaipur Wall Art Festival

5 February — 20 February 2025

Old City, Jaipur

Murals engaging with Jaipur's architectural heritage and Rajasthani folk art traditions.

  • Murals across walled city
  • Heritage walks
  • Local artist showcase

Organiser: Jaipur Art Foundation

UPCOMING

Hyderabad Urban Art Summit

1 March — 10 March 2025

Hyderabad

Festival exploring South Indian visual traditions with contemporary street art practice.

  • 12+ murals
  • Kalamkari demonstrations
  • Artist residency

Organiser: Deccan Art Collective
