ABOUT RANGREZZ STREET

Home > About

WHO WE ARE

Rangrezz Street is an independent digital platform dedicated to documenting, celebrating, and amplifying the world of Indian street art and graffiti culture. We are not a gallery, not a brand, and not a government initiative. We are a passion project — built by people who believe that the most honest art in India is not hanging in a museum. It is painted on a wall in broad daylight for everyone to see.

The name Rangrezz comes from an Urdu and Hindi word meaning one who colours — a dyer, a painter, someone who transforms the ordinary into something vivid and alive. That is exactly what Indian street artists do to the grey concrete of our cities. And that is exactly what we are trying to do with this website.

OUR MISSION

🎨

DOCUMENT

India's street art is temporary by nature. Walls get repainted, buildings get demolished, murals fade. Our mission is to create a permanent digital record of Indian street art before it disappears — artist by artist, city by city, wall by wall.

📢

AMPLIFY

Most Indian street artists work without the support of galleries, agents, or institutional backing. Rangrezz Street exists to give these artists a wider audience — to put their work in front of people who would never have discovered it otherwise.

🤝

CONNECT

We believe street art creates community. Rangrezz Street connects artists with audiences, cities with visitors, and tradition with the contemporary. We are building a community around Indian public art that does not exist anywhere else.

OUR STORY

Rangrezz Street started with a single photograph taken on a phone camera in Lodhi Colony, Delhi. A mural so powerful it stopped us in our tracks — a massive Devanagari word painted so that only its shadow could be read at a specific time of day. We stood there for twenty minutes just watching the shadow move.

That moment raised a question that would not go away: how many walls like this exist across India that nobody knows about? How many artists are doing extraordinary work in cities that never make it into any newspaper, any gallery, any conversation?

Rangrezz Street is our answer to that question. We started documenting. We started reaching out to artists. We started building what we wished already existed — a comprehensive, honest, and genuinely passionate record of Indian street art culture.

WHY RANGREZZ STREET IS DIFFERENT

There are plenty of websites about street art. Most of them are about Western street art — New York, London, Berlin. Indian street art is treated as a footnote, a curiosity, a lesser cousin of the real thing. We disagree with that completely. Indian street art has its own history going back thousands of years through cave paintings, temple murals, and folk traditions. It has its own visual language shaped by Devanagari script, Bollywood iconography, Madhubani line work, and the chaotic energy of Indian urban life. It has its own artists doing work that is as original and as powerful as anything happening anywhere in the world. Rangrezz Street exists to make that case — loudly, visually, and without apology.
